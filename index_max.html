Chimney Swifts

The chimney swift, a bird native to Ontario, has been designated as a threatened species by the province of Ontario as of September, 2009 [1] and are tracked by government and non-profit organizations. These birds naturally nest and roost on cave walls and in hollow trees, but following the emergence of the man-made chimney, utilize open-ended chimneys for this purpose. SwiftWatch aims to help non-profit organizations by counting their video feed for them, eliminating manual counting.

Background subtraction

Separating the foreground from the background.

SwiftWatch performs a background subtraction method to each frame, providing you the option of using two different types of Gaussian Mixture-based Background/Foreground Segmentation Algorithms [2], MOG or MOG2. MOG is a background subtraction algorithm based on a paper [3] written by P. KadewTraKuPong and R. Bowden and MOG2 is a background subtraction algorithm based on two papers [4][5] written by Z.Zivkovic.

Background subtraction works by simply taking a frame without any objects (birds) and subtracting it from each new frame as you iterate through the selected video. This allows SwiftWatch to get the foreground objects (birds) without background noise being introduced [2].

Object tracking

Bird multitracking at its best.

Tracking is best accomplished with a Minimum Output Sum of Squared Error or MOSSE tracker, exemplified by the lowest average error and the smallest range of error. The MOSSE tracker performs best due to its support for deformable objects and occlusion [6]. Extreme occlusion occurs when a swarm of swifts enter the chimney together.

Another tracker offered as an option is the Discriminative Correlation Filters (DCF) with Channel and Spatial Reliability or CSRT tracker. CSRT tracking is a learning algorithm that provides efficient and seamless integration in the filter update and the tracking process. The spatial reliability map provides support to the object that is being tracked. In doing so, tracking of non-rectangular objects is improved and allows for search regions to be enlarged [7].

Export your count

Documenting your count for you.

Document your counts by exporting your results to a comma separated values (CSV) file to allow for data preservation. Upon export, three columns are created with the following information written to it: {file name}, hh:mm:ss, {count value per second}.

Launch your App

SwiftWatch eliminates the reliance on manual counting and replaces it with an automated system, utilizing a computer vision approach. The completed approach writes each count to a CSV file with a name of the video, timestamp in hours:minutes:seconds, and swift entering count. SwiftWatch allows you to save a considerable amount of time by eliminating manual counting altogether.
